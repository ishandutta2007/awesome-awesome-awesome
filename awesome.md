# The Awesome Awesome Awesome Manifesto

## What makes a resource "awesome" for this list?

This is not a list of everything that exists. It is a **curated signal** for technologists working on the problems that will define the next several decades.

An entry is awesome if it meets these criteria:

### 1. Forward trajectory
The tool, framework, or resource must be relevant to technologies with significant impact in 2025–2040. Web development frameworks, legacy enterprise software, and maintenance-mode tools are out of scope regardless of their quality.

### 2. Real-world adoption
Entries must be used by real researchers, engineers, or companies — not just theoretical proposals. GitHub stars are one signal but not the only one. A seminal paper with 10,000 citations belongs here even if it has no code.

### 3. Honest descriptions
Descriptions explain why something belongs here — not just what it is. Bad: "A library for X." Good: "The standard tool for X in research; used by Y and Z for production deployments."

### 4. Completeness over brevity
Where other awesome lists have 10 entries per domain, this list aims for depth. A robotics engineer should be able to use this as a genuine starting point for any sub-domain.

### 5. Cross-domain awareness
The coming decades are defined by **convergence** — AI × biology, quantum × materials, robotics × simulation. Entries that enable cross-domain work are especially valued.
